Founders assume the build is the slow part. It rarely is. The slow part is everything that happens because a decision was postponed.

## The real timeline killers
A feature gets built, shown to the founder, and rebuilt because it was never specified. That round trip costs a week. It happens four times and you have lost a month.

Nobody decides who the first user is, so the product tries to serve three audiences at once. Every screen gets more complicated. Every complication adds days.

## What a six week build looks like
Week one is discovery. We come out of it with a written spec, a fixed feature list, and a clear answer to what happens if we run out of time.

Weeks two and three are design and the first working screens. You see it, you react to it, and we adjust before anything is expensive to change.

Weeks four and five are the build. Weekly demos, so surprises surface early.

Week six is launch, plus the tuning that always comes after real users touch it.

## The uncomfortable part
Shipping in six weeks means cutting things. The founders who ship fastest are the ones who can say no to their own ideas for eight weeks.

## What to cut first
Anything a user can do manually for the first month. Anything that only matters at scale you do not have yet. Every admin screen you can replace with a spreadsheet until version two.
